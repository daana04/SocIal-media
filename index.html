Social Media and Mental Health

While many of us enjoy staying connected on social media, excessive use can fuel feelings of addiction, anxiety, depression, isolation, and FOMO. Here’s how to modify your habits and improve your mood.

The negative aspects of Social Media

Social Media Addiction.

Face-to-face contact is vital for mental health. Eye-to-eye connections with caring individuals quickly reduce stress and enhance mood. Prioritizing in-person connections over social media can prevent mood disorders.

Read More

Isolation.

A University of Pennsylvania study revealed that extensive use of Facebook, Snapchat, and Instagram heightens loneliness. Conversely, reducing social media enhances well-being by alleviating loneliness and isolation.

Read More

Cyberbullying.

Approximately 10% of teens experience social media bullying, with offensive comments affecting numerous users. Platforms like Twitter can harbor hurtful rumors, lies, and abuse, causing lasting emotional wounds.

Read More

What’s driving your Social Media use?

These days, most of us access social media via our smartphones or tablets. While this makes it very convenient to keep in touch, it also means that social media is always accessible. This round-the-clock, hyper connectivity can trigger impulse control problems, the constant alerts and notifications affecting your concentration and focus, disturbing your sleep, and making you a slave to your phone.

Social media platforms are designed to snare your attention, keep you online, and have you repeatedly checking your screen for updates. It’s how the companies make money. But, much like a gambling compulsion or an addiction to nicotine, alcohol, or drugs, social media use can create psychological cravings.
